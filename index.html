    Colours

    Colour defines the emotional meaning and depth of the website - all based on the emotions they evoke within us. This makes it all the more important to ensure that the correct combination and amount of colour is used in your websites. Contrast is key to the overall design of a website. The one thing to remember is as much as they're beautiful, colours can make - or break - your website. And that isn't going to be a pretty site.

    The swatches above are examples of two colours brightened and darkened from a single colour, SpringGreen, but contrasted effectively to build an atmosphere of harmony while emphasising key information on a site. This combination could be used in a site based around nature and conservation, possibly suggesting we should live in harmony with nature. It also provides a harmonious light and dark theme to switch between that can be implemented on the site.

    Typography

    Typography is the art of styling and arranging text on any medium that has been designed to convey information. It is affects how we take in information and whether we will remember it enough to be willing to visit the website again.

    Finding fonts that fit seamlessly together can be quite a challenge, given the vast amount of different fonts at out disposal. As a general rule of thumb, never use more than three fonts on a website unless there is no other way around it. A good resource to use is this collection of Google Font Pairings which will save you a lot of time.

    These fonts are all taken from Google Fonts, but you may use any resource you wish.

    Buttons

    Buttons provide an interactive feature to navigate in and outside of a website. They should be intuitive and distinguishable from normal text to add to the comfort the website should provide.

    There are a different styles you can add to a button, including neumorphism, skeuomorphism, flat and ghost, and material. In this site, we will be using flat and ghost.
